INTRESTS

These are my top 5 Survivor seasons:

#3- Survivor: Cagayan- Brains vs Brawn vs Beauty (Season 28)

Survivor Cagayan is a great season, mainly because there were so many legendary players. This season saw the first appearances of tony, Spencer, Tasha, Kass, and Sarah, who all did extremely well when they played subsequent times. Even people who left early, like J'Tia and Brice, were still fun and memorable in their own way. The chaotic strategic gameplay used in this season, as well as its rootable underdogs, make this season one for the books.

#3- Survivor: Kaoh Rong- Brains vs Brawn vs Beauty (Season 32)

After the success of Cagayan, the producers decided to try out the Brains vs Brawn vs Beauty format again. WHile the gameplay wasnt as dynamic, the cast was amazing. Unfortunately, this ended up being the most dangerous season, with three medical evacuations (still currently the record for any one season). That being said, it has one of the most exciting final fours of all time, and its filled to the brim with people you love, and people you love to hate.

#3- Survivor: Winners at War (Season 40)

This season had hands down, the best cast of all time. As the title of the season implies, this cast is made up of people who won the game before. Watching the best of the best duke it out was amazing, and helped me get through the first few weeks of quarantine. It had an exciting pre-merge and early merge period, but overall it came a bit less exciting, as one player pretty much dominated the endgame, with a few other just not really doing much exciting. That being said, the caliber of the cast definitely made up for it, and it was nice to see certain people play again after all these years.

#2- Survivor: Cambodia- Second Chance (Season 31)

This was the most strategically intense season of all time. What made this season different from the others before it is that the cast was voted in by the fans. I believe this is the factor that cranked everything to 11. Everyone was playing their hearts out, and used their strengths to their best capabilities. That, combined with an unusually large merge period, made for some bold gameplay, and an ultimately satisfying final three that made it to the end of the game.

#1- Survivor: David vs Goliath (Season 37)

While Winners at War had the best cast of all time, this season had the best cast of all new players ever. Everyone felt like a dynamic and interesting person, and the show's edit showcased it very well. This was everyone's first attempt at playing, but they played with the competency of veterans. The flashy moves of the early merge combined with the ever shitfting power dynamics for the rest of the season make this a very enjoyable watch.
